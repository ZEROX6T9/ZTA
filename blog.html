Orion Nebula
I took my first nebula photo. It's so bad but it is a golden picture for me... But the Orion Nebula is a star nursery located in the constellation Orion, known for its bright and active star formation. It is part of the Orion Molecular Cloud Complex and is one of the most intense areas of stellar formation within the Milky Way Galaxy. The nebula is best observed in January and is visible to the naked eye with an apparent magnitude of 4.0. It contains a young open cluster of four primary stars known as the Trapezium, which are clustered about a nexus of four massive stars. The Trapezium stars are known for their intense radiation, which excites the nebula to glow. The Orion Nebula has been a subject of study for centuries and continues to be a key target for astronomers studying stellar birth and evolution.

Pleiades – Seven Sisters
The Seven Sisters, known as the Pleiades, is a prominent star cluster in the constellation Taurus, famous for its beauty and rich mythology... The Pleiades, or Seven Sisters, is an open star cluster consisting of more than 1,000 stars, located approximately 410 light-years from Earth. It is one of the nearest star clusters to our planet and is easily visible to the naked eye, especially during the fall and winter months in the Northern Hemisphere.
